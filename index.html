Learn an Instrument

Pick one instrument, learn sargams using free online resources, practice daily, and play a recognizable composition by week 4. Creative expression unlocked.

Keyboard / Ukulele / Flute Sargam Sa Re Ga Ma Composition YouTube & Apps Daily Practice

🎯 Step Zero: Choose Your Instrument

Pick ONE. Commit for 4 weeks. Here are the best beginner-friendly options:

InstrumentCostDifficultyWhy It's Great for Beginners
🎹 Keyboard (Casio)₹3,000-5,000EasyVisual layout, sargam maps directly to keys, no tuning needed
🎸 Ukulele₹1,500-3,000EasyOnly 4 strings, songs within days, portable, fun sound
🪈 Flute (Bansuri)₹200-800MediumIndian classical roots, breath control, cheapest option
🎸 Guitar₹3,000-5,000Medium-HardMost versatile, but finger pain in week 1-2, slower progress
🥁 Tabla (pair)₹3,000-6,000HardRhythm mastery, Indian classical, but needs more time

💡 Recommendation: Keyboard or Ukulele — fastest path to playing real music in 4 weeks.

Week 1 — Basics: Sargam, Posture & First Notes

🎵 Day 1-2: Indian Music Theory (Sargam)

  • The 7 Swaras: Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa — YouTube: Sargam Explained (Hindi)
  • Saptak: Mandra (lower), Madhya (middle), Taar (upper octave)
  • Shuddh vs Komal vs Teevra — YouTube: Indian Music Basics
  • Listen: YouTube: All 12 Notes of Indian Music
  • Sing: try singing Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa — match pitch with video
  • Understand: Thaat (scale), Raag (melody framework) — just awareness level
📌 Activity: Sing the full ascending & descending sargam (Aaroh-Avroh) and record yourself

🖐️ Day 3-4: Instrument Setup & First Notes

  • Get your instrument — buy or borrow (keyboard: even a ₹2K Casio works)
  • YouTube beginner series for your instrument:
  • — Keyboard: The Piano Keys | Bollywood Piano Tutorials
  • — Ukulele: The Ukulele Teacher
  • — Flute: Bansuri Tutorials (Hindi)
  • Learn: proper hand position, posture, how to produce clean notes
  • Play: Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa on your instrument — slow is fine
📌 Activity: Play the ascending sargam (Sa to Sa) cleanly — even if very slow

🔁 Day 5-7: Practice Routine & Finger Training

  • Daily practice structure (30 min):
  • — 5 min: warm up (scales up and down slowly)
  • — 15 min: lesson/exercise from YouTube tutorial
  • — 10 min: free play / repeat what sounds good
  • Aaroh-Avroh: ascending and descending scales — smooth transitions
  • Speed: start at 60 BPM — use Metronome Online or app "Pro Metronome" (free)
  • Apps: Yousician (free tier) | Perfect Ear (ear training)
📌 Activity: Play ascending + descending sargam at steady tempo — record day 1 vs day 7 comparison
Week 2 — Simple Melodies & Alankars

🎶 Day 8-10: Alankars (Ornamental Patterns)

  • Alankar 1: Sa Re, Re Ga, Ga Ma, Ma Pa... (step pattern)
  • Alankar 2: Sa Re Ga, Re Ga Ma, Ga Ma Pa... (3-note pattern)
  • Alankar 3: Sa Re Ga Ma, Re Ga Ma Pa... (4-note pattern)
  • Practice each alankar ascending AND descending
  • Purpose: builds finger memory, speed, smooth transitions
  • Increase tempo gradually: 60 → 70 → 80 BPM over the week
📌 Activity: Play all 3 alankars smoothly — ascending and descending without pausing

🎵 Day 11-12: Your First Song (Sargam Notation)

  • Pick a simple Bollywood song in sargam notation:
  • — "Kal Ho Na Ho" — YouTube: Kal Ho Na Ho Sargam
  • — "Tujhe Dekha Toh" — YouTube: Tujhe Dekha Toh Notes
  • — "Jana Gana Mana" — YouTube: National Anthem Keyboard
  • YouTube search: "[your song] sargam notation keyboard/flute"
  • Play note by note first, then connect into melody
  • Don't worry about speed — accuracy first, speed comes naturally
📌 Activity: Learn one recognizable melody — play it start to finish (even slowly)

👂 Day 13-14: Ear Training & Rhythm

  • Ear training: listen to a note, try to identify (Sa? Re? Ga?)
  • App: "Perfect Ear" — free exercises for note recognition
  • Rhythm: basic Taal patterns — Dadra (6 beats), Keherwa (8 beats)
  • Clap along: Dha Dhin Na | Dha Dhin Na (Dadra 6-beat cycle)
  • Play your sargam exercises IN rhythm — with metronome
  • Listen actively: put on any song, try to identify the Sa (root note)
📌 Activity: Identify the sargam of a simple song by ear (even 4-5 notes) — write it down
Week 3 — Building Repertoire & Expression

🎼 Day 15-18: Learn 2-3 More Compositions

  • Add 2 more songs to your repertoire — varied moods:
  • — 1 slow/emotional: "Tere Bina" or "Tum Hi Ho" intro
  • — 1 upbeat: "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" or a folk tune
  • — 1 classical: any simple Raag Yaman or Bhairav composition
  • For each song: first learn sargam notation, then play slowly, then build speed
  • Practice transitions between songs — build a mini "setlist"
  • Focus on expression: where to pause, where to slow down, dynamics
📌 Activity: Play 3 different melodies back-to-back without stopping — your first mini-concert!

🎨 Day 19-21: Creative Expression & Improvisation

  • Pick a Raag (e.g., Yaman: Sa Re Ga Ma# Pa Dha Ni Sa)
  • Play only those notes — improvise a melody within the raag
  • This is COMPOSITION: you're creating music, not just reproducing
  • Record your improvisation — listen back, what sounds nice?
  • Try: play the same phrase happy vs sad (tempo + dynamics change)
  • Music theory: why some notes sound good together (consonance vs dissonance)
📌 Activity: Compose a 30-second original melody using sargam — record it
Week 4 — Polish, Perform & Reflect

✨ Day 22-25: Polish Your Best Piece

  • Choose your best composition/song — the one you'll perform
  • Practice it 10+ times daily — aim for zero mistakes
  • Work on: smooth transitions, consistent tempo, clean notes
  • Add expression: soft parts, loud parts, slight pauses for drama
  • Record yourself: watch/listen, fix weak spots
  • Compare your day 1 recording vs now — celebrate the progress!
📌 Activity: Record a "final take" of your performance piece — clean, confident, expressive

🎤 Day 26-28: Live Performance & Reflection

  • Perform for family/friends: play your best piece + 1-2 others
  • Share: post a short clip on social media or WhatsApp status
  • Teach: explain sargam to someone — "here's how Sa Re Ga Ma works"
  • Reflect: what did music teach you about patience and practice?
  • Future plan: will you continue? Next steps (YouTube series, online class)
  • Connection to teaching: how can music/rhythm help in your classroom?
📌 Activity: Perform live for at least 2 people + share a video recording of your best piece
